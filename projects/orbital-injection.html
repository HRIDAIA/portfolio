Optimal Guidance and Feedback Control for Orbital Injection

This case study extends the AE6511 Optimal Guidance & Control project brief (Bryson & Ho, §6.4) that maximizes horizontal velocity during a thrust-direction burn. The reference problem assumes a constant-mass, constant-gravity rocket that must reach altitude h at time T with zero vertical velocity while preserving the highest possible downrange speed.

I’m translating the analytical specification into a modern tooling stack—symbolic solvers for the Euler-Lagrange equations, convex approximations for guidance tables, and Monte Carlo + feedback studies that shake out dispersions before feeding the results into my multi-agent simulation framework.

Spec Overview

  • States: x (downrange), z (altitude), u (horizontal velocity), w (vertical velocity); dynamics ẋ = u, ż = w, ū = a cos β, ẇ = a sin β − g.
  • Control: thrust direction β measured counterclockwise from the x-axis; acceleration magnitude a is constant with a/g = 3 and non-dimensionalized 2h/(aT²) = 0.258.
  • Objective: maximize terminal horizontal velocity (equivalently minimize J = −a ∫₀ᴺ cos β dt) subject to x(0)=z(0)=u(0)=w(0)=0, z(T)=h, w(T)=0, and free x(T), u(T).
  • Tasks from the brief: derive Euler-Lagrange and boundary conditions, solve for the optimal β(t), plot state/costate trajectories and Hamiltonian, verify constraints, and build perturbation feedback gains.

Planned Additions

  • Numerically integrate the necessary conditions, validate against Bryson & Ho’s closed-form thrust steering law, and package the solution in a MATLAB/Python notebook.
  • Compute second-order optimality checks and neighboring-extremal feedback gains, then visualize gain schedules across the burn.
  • Inject 0.5g sinusoidal acceleration disturbances to compare open-loop, feedforward, and feedback-compensated trajectories.
  • Publish scripts, figures, and guidance tables so the scenario can slot directly into larger ascent/autonomy experiments.
